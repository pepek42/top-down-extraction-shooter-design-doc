  - Issues

    - For me game has too much micromanagement for 6+ squads

  - Cons
    - At times feels too minimalistic, like weapons selection

    - Progression scaling is a bit off - only very well-balanced in the mid-game

## Looting issue

A lot of extraction looter shooter games have very similar issues IMO. At the beginning looting feels good, you find items, but in mid-late game looting becomes issue.

Main problem are

* A lot of loot becomes garbage, that at best you need to collect and sell to have money. Feels like busy job. Ok, so let's have bitcoin farm and so, now loot is even more useless
* Hunting rare items for quests/upgrades is nightmare... But I do understand that finding that item, tension to extract and dopamine if successful
* Locations are spacious enough to accommodate all that loot, this creates even more downtime walking around

## Hiring NPCs

A lot of those games would benefit from ability to hire/recruit NPCs into squad. You can really open game design into more complicated, exciting fights.

Main issue with that I assume is really hard to make competent enough allies that will help you instead of interrupting you, see Thunder Tier One I guess. Alternative is to micromange them as in Door Kickers.
